About this Site

Why this exists, and where the material comes from.

About the project

This site is a midterm project for a Web and Cloud Technology course, built from plain HTML and CSS without a framework. Marcus Aurelius was the subject because his version of Stoicism — short, private, written under real pressure on a military campaign — holds up well outside a classroom.

Sources

  • Meditations. Written by Marcus Aurelius, c. 170–180 AD. The translation quoted throughout this site is by George Long (1862), now in the public domain, via the Internet Classics Archive at MIT.
  • Biographical details — dates, offices, and the outline of the Parthian and Marcomannic Wars — follow the standard historical record of the Antonine period.

About the images

The laurel medallion used throughout this site is an original line drawing, not a photograph. Ancient Rome represented its emperors through coins and engraved portraits rather than photography, so a medallion mark felt closer to the source material than a stock photo would.
