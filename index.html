Marcus Aurelius

121 – 180 AD

Marcus Aurelius ruled Rome at the height of its power, yet spent most of his reign at war on the empire's northern frontier. Between campaigns, he kept a private notebook of moral reminders to himself — a Stoic philosopher's exercise in staying clear-headed under pressure. That notebook survived. It is known today as the Meditations.

The Life

Born into the Roman elite, named heir at seventeen, and worn down by two decades of war on the frontier.

The Philosophy

Four Stoic ideas he returned to again and again: control, virtue, mortality, and duty.

The Words

Eight lines from his private notebook, with the book each one comes from.

The things which are external to my mind have no relation at all to my mind.
Meditations, Book 7
