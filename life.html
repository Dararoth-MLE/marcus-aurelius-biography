121 – 180 AD

The Life of Marcus Aurelius

From a senatorial childhood in Rome to two decades commanding legions on the Danube.

  1. 121

    Born in Rome

    Marcus Annius Verus is born in Rome on 26 April, into a wealthy senatorial family with roots in southern Spain.

  2. 138

    Named heir

    At Emperor Hadrian's instruction, Antoninus Pius adopts Marcus as his son and heir. He takes the name Marcus Aurelius and continues his training in law, rhetoric, and philosophy, studying under tutors that include the Stoic Junius Rusticus.

  3. 145

    Marriage

    Marcus marries Faustina the Younger, daughter of Antoninus Pius. The couple have at least thirteen children over the following decades, though few survive to adulthood.

  4. 161

    Becomes emperor

    Antoninus Pius dies. Marcus becomes emperor and immediately names his adoptive brother Lucius Verus as co-emperor — the first time Rome is ruled by two men at once.

  5. 161–166

    War in the east

    Parthia invades Roman territory in the east. Lucius Verus leads the campaign that pushes them back, while the legions returning from it carry a plague that will spread through the empire for years.

  6. 166–180

    War on the Danube

    Germanic tribes cross the frontier in the north. Marcus spends most of the rest of his reign on campaign along the Danube, governing the empire from military camps rather than from Rome.

  7. 170s

    Writing to himself

    On campaign, Marcus keeps a private notebook of short, unpolished reflections — reminders to himself on how to stay steady, fair, and useful. He never intends it for publication.

  8. 180

    Death on campaign

    Marcus dies in March, still on campaign on the northern frontier. His son Commodus succeeds him, ending the run of capable emperors later historians would call the "Five Good Emperors."
